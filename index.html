‚úÇÔ∏è CRISPR-Cas9

CRISPR is a revolutionary gene editing tool that works like molecular scissors:

  • Guide RNA (gRNA): Directs Cas9 to the target DNA sequence
  • Cas9 protein: Cuts the DNA at the specified location
  • DNA repair: Cell fixes the cut (often introducing changes)
üéØ CRISPR Applications

Knockout: Disrupt a gene | Knock-in: Insert new DNA | Base editing: Change single letters

Phage Therapeutics
Viruses that kill bacteria

ü¶† What Are Bacteriophages?

Bacteriophages (phages) are viruses that specifically infect and kill bacteria. They are:

  • The most abundant biological entities on Earth
  • Highly specific (each targets certain bacteria)
  • Self-replicating when inside their host

üíä Why Phage Therapy?

The antibiotic resistance crisis is a major global health threat:

  • 1.2M+ deaths/year from antibiotic-resistant infections
  • Few new antibiotics in development
  • Phages offer a targeted alternative
‚ö° Phage vs Antibiotics

Antibiotics: Broad-spectrum, resistance spreads easily
Phages: Highly specific, co-evolve with bacteria

‚ö° What Are Genetic Circuits?

Genetic circuits are engineered DNA sequences that perform logical operations in living cells, similar to electronic circuits.

üíª Code Analogy

Promoters = function calls

üéØ Example: Toggle Switch

A genetic toggle switch can exist in two stable states (ON or OFF), just like a light switch. It was one of the first synthetic genetic circuits built (Gardner et al., 2000).

Genetic circuits can be designed as biosensors for aging biomarkers or as therapeutic systems that respond to cellular aging states.
